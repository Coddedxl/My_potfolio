MRI Alzheimer's disease Analysis

This dashboard presents a neuroimaging analysis focused on distinguishing between healthy brain scans and varying stages of neurological disease progression. Using MRI-derived features such as pixel intensity, entropy levels, radiance progression, and brightness variation, the analysis translates complex medical imaging data into interpretable metrics. The goal is to uncover patterns that signal early-stage cognitive decline and differentiate them from normal brain aging. This type of analysis is highly relevant in modern healthcare, where data-driven diagnostics can support clinicians in making faster, more accurate decisions and ultimately improve patient outcomes.

The core problem:
addressed here is the difficulty of consistently identifying subtle structural and intensity-based differences in brain scans across disease stages. Traditional visual inspection is time-consuming and subject to human bias, especially when differences between “very mild” and “mild” conditions are barely perceptible. Without a structured analytical approach, early detection becomes unreliable, delaying intervention and reducing the chances of effective treatment. Additionally, large volumes of imaging data make it impractical to manually extract meaningful insights without automation and clear visualization.
To solve this:
I designed a data-driven dashboard that combines statistical analysis with visual storytelling. I engineered key metrics such as disease progression index, scan disorder levels, and entropy distributions to quantify variations across scan categories. By integrating comparative visuals—like pixel intensity distributions and structural clarity rankings—I enabled quick identification of anomalies between healthy and affected scans. The inclusion of “Top Disease” and “Top Healthy” scan samples further bridges the gap between raw data and real-world interpretation, allowing users to visually validate the patterns highlighted in the metrics.

    Tesla Stock Sales Analysis

    This dashboard provides a clear overview of Tesla vehicle market performance, pricing trends, mileage distribution, financing patterns, and accident history across different states in the United States. The analysis reveals that the average Tesla selling price is approximately $45.11K, while the median odometer reading stands at 28.67K miles, showing that most vehicles in the dataset are moderately used.

    What factors influence the value and condition of Tesla vehicles in the resale market?
    To answer this, the analysis explored vehicle prices, mileage, financing behavior, accident history, and geographic trends. At first glance, the market appears healthy because most Tesla vehicles recorded no accident history. This immediately suggests that buyers still maintain trust in Tesla vehicles and that resale quality remains strong.

There were also small errors in numbers and labels that could confuse anyone reading the dashboard. If we used the data like that, the results would not be correct and stakeholders could make wrong decisions.
